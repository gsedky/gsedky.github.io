ENAE 675

Classical theories of incompressible unsteady aerodynamics with an introduction to modeling techniques. Topics include: unsteady Bernoulli's equation, added mass, the indicial response method, dynamic stall, and modern applications.

Steady Panel Method

The steady panel method is a classic computational tool used to model steady flow around thin airfoils. It has been historically used in aircraft design. This lecture teaches the basics of how to develop your own steady panel code for airfoils with arbitrary camber.

Unsteady Panel Method

The steady panel method is extended by introducing vortex shedding as well a time marching to capture unsteady flow effects. The wing sheds vortex elements from the trailing edge at every time step, subject to enforcing the kutta condition as well as Kelvin's circulation theorem. The vorticity in the wing's wake is allowed to freely convect.

Theordorsen's Unsteady Aerodynamics Theory

Theodosen's unsteady lift theory models the evolution of lift for a harmonically pitching or plunging airfoil. In this series of lectures, we derive the theory and demonstrate its use and physical significance.

Wagner's and Küssner's Unsteady Aerodynamics Theory

Wagner's model captures the lift experienced by a wing subject to a step response in pitch or surge. On the other hand, Küssner's model captures the lift experienced by a wing encoureing a transverse step gust. In this lecture, we explore both methods.
