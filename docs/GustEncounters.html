Gust Encounter Experiments







The flow physics spurred by wings encountering latge-amplitude tranverse gusts are very rich. The flow fully separates from the wing's suction side, and a strong leading-edge vortex (LEV) forms. The LEV induces very low pressures on the upper surface of the wing, leading to lift augmentation. In addition to that, the gust velocity affects the wing's wake, leading to further modifications to the forces felt by the wing. Finally, the gust and the wing are aerodynamially coupled. The presence of the wing in the gust leads to significant deformation in the gust structure. In my work, I study the aerodynamics of wing-gust encounter exerimentally. I use force measurments to acquire the aerodynamic loads, and high-speed particle image velocimtery to record the planar flowfield velocity around the wing. Sample PIV gifs are shown above. In addition, I investigate the impact of various pitching maneeuvers on mitigating the lift impact of the gust. The video below shows the flowfield and force results of various maneuvers. The open-loop pitch maneuvers were acquired based on classical unsteady aerodynamic models as well as closed-loop discrete vortex simulations.
