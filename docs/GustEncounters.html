Gust Encounter Experiments

The flow physics spurred by wings encountering latge-amplitude tranverse gusts are very rich. the flow fully separated from the wing's suction side, and a strong leading-edge vortex (LEV) forms. The LEV induces very low pressures on the upper surface of the wing, leading to lift augmentation. In addition to that, the gust velocity affects the wing's wake, leading to further modifications to the forces felt by the wing. Finally, the gust and the wing are aerodynamially coupled. The presence of the wing in the gust leads to significant deformation in the gust structure. In my work, I study the aerodynamics of wing-gust encounter exerimentally. I used force measurments to acquire the aerodynamic loads, and high-speed particle image velocimtery to record the planar flowfield velocity around the wing.
